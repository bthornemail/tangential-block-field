# Residual Field — The Surface of Disjointness and Conflict

The Residual Field ($\mathbb{R} \subseteq \mathbb{B}$) records unresolved incidence rather than allowing information to be silently discarded or automatically interpreted as failure [0x01.9].

This provides a concrete computational meaning to standard error ($STDERR = \text{residual incidence field}$) [0x01.9]. At the geometric level, it is the surface containing unresolved overlap, ambiguity, projection mismatch, failed closure, ownership conflicts, and rejected incidences [0x01.10].
